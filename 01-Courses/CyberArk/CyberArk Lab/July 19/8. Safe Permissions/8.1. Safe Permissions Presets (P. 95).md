#cyberark-labs 


![[permissions-presets.png]]

These **Permission Presets** are simply **pre-made permission packages**. Instead of checking 30+ boxes one by one, CyberArk lets you choose a preset based on the person's job.

Think of it like a video game.

Instead of giving every player individual abilities, you choose a class:

- 🧙 Wizard
    
- 🛡️ Knight
    
- 🏹 Archer
    

Each class already has the right abilities.

CyberArk does the same thing.

---

# What is a Permission Preset?

Imagine you're creating a new Safe called:

```
Production Servers
```

Now you want to give Bob access.

CyberArk asks:

> "What kind of user is Bob?"

Instead of manually selecting 30 permissions, you click one preset.

Example:

```
Bob
↓

Read Only
```

CyberArk automatically checks the appropriate permission boxes.

---

# 1. Connect Only

Think of this person as **someone who only needs to log in to servers**.

### Real-life example

A Windows administrator needs to fix a server.

They don't need to:

- Add accounts
    
- Delete accounts
    
- Change Safe settings
    

They only need to connect.

CyberArk gives them:

✅ List Accounts

✅ Use Accounts

❌ Retrieve Password

❌ Delete Accounts

❌ Manage Safe

---

### What can they do?

```
CyberArk
     │
     ▼
Click Connect
     │
     ▼
PSM
     │
     ▼
Windows Server
```

They can work on the server.

But they never see the password.

---

### Why do we need it?

This is the **most secure way** for administrators to work.

Even if someone asks:

> "What's the Administrator password?"

The user honestly cannot tell them—they never saw it.

---

## Think of it like...

A hotel guest.

They have a key card.

They can enter the room.

But they cannot make new key cards or change the locks.

---

# 2. Read Only

This person can **look**, but not **change** anything.

Think of them as a visitor in a museum.

They can see everything.

They cannot touch anything.

Usually they have:

✅ List Accounts

✅ Retrieve Password

❌ Add Accounts

❌ Delete Accounts

❌ Manage Safe

---

### Example

An auditor wants to inspect privileged accounts.

They can:

```
See accounts

Read passwords (if allowed)

Read information
```

But they cannot modify anything.

---

### Why do we need it?

Sometimes people only need to inspect information.

They should not accidentally change anything.

---

## Think of it like...

Reading a book in a library.

You can read it.

You cannot rewrite it.

---

# 3. Approver

Some companies require permission before someone can use an important account.

Example

Tim wants to use:

```
Domain Administrator
```

CyberArk says

```
Approval Required
```

The request goes to the manager.

The manager clicks

```
Approve
```

Now Tim can connect.

---

The Approver preset mainly includes:

✅ View Requests

✅ Approve Requests

❌ Delete Accounts

❌ Manage Safe

---

### Why do we need it?

Very powerful accounts should not be used whenever someone feels like it.

Someone must approve access.

---

## Think of it like...

A teacher signing a permission slip before a student goes on a school trip.

Without the signature...

No trip.

---

# 4. Accounts Manager

This is usually someone from the CyberArk team.

Their job is to **manage accounts** inside the Safe.

They can:

✅ Add Accounts

✅ Rename Accounts

✅ Delete Accounts

✅ Update Properties

✅ Trigger CPM

But they usually **don't manage the Safe itself**.

---

### Example

A new Linux server is installed.

The Account Manager adds:

```
root

linuxadmin

backupuser
```

to the Safe.

Later they update:

```
IP Address

Platform

Username

Port
```

---

### Why do we need it?

Companies constantly add:

- New servers
    
- New databases
    
- New service accounts
    

Someone has to manage those accounts.

---

## Think of it like...

A librarian.

They organize books.

Add books.

Remove books.

Rename shelves.

But they don't own the library.

---

# 5. Full

This is the highest level of permissions.

Think of this person as the **owner of the Safe**.

They can do everything.

✅ Connect

✅ Retrieve Passwords

✅ Add Accounts

✅ Delete Accounts

✅ Manage Members

✅ Manage Safe

✅ View Logs

✅ Create Folders

✅ Delete Folders

Everything.

---

### Example

A CyberArk Administrator creates a Safe.

They can:

```
Create Safe

↓

Add Members

↓

Add Accounts

↓

Change Permissions

↓

Delete Accounts

↓

View Logs

↓

Manage Everything
```

---

### Why do we need it?

Someone has to own and manage the Safe.

Otherwise nobody could:

- Add users
    
- Change permissions
    
- Maintain the Safe
    

---

## Think of it like...

The principal of a school.

The principal can:

- Hire teachers
    
- Remove teachers
    
- Change school rules
    
- Open classrooms
    
- Lock classrooms
    

They control everything.

# Easy Way to Remember

|Preset|Think of them as...|Main Job|
|---|---|---|
|🔌 **Connect Only**|Server Administrator|Connect to servers through PSM without seeing passwords|
|👀 **Read Only**|Auditor or Viewer|View accounts and information without making changes|
|✅ **Approver**|Manager|Approve or reject requests to use privileged accounts|
|📚 **Accounts Manager**|CyberArk Account Administrator|Add, update, rename, and manage accounts in the Safe|
|👑 **Full**|Safe Owner / CyberArk Administrator|Complete control over the Safe, its accounts, and its members|

## One important thing to know

These presets are **just starting templates**. When you choose **Connect Only** or **Accounts Manager**, CyberArk automatically checks a group of permission boxes for you.

You can then switch to **Custom** and change individual permissions if your company needs something different.

For example, you might start with **Accounts Manager**, then remove **Delete Accounts** because your company's security policy doesn't allow account managers to delete accounts. This flexibility is why many organizations end up using **Custom** permissions even though they start from one of the built-in presets.
