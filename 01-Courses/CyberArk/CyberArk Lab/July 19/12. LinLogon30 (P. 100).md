Think of the **`SessionRecorderSafe`** parameter as the label on a specific drawer in a filing cabinet. Here is the breakdown of what it is and how it works:

### 1. What is its purpose?

The purpose of the `SessionRecorderSafe` value is to give CyberArk a **dedicated "folder" (called a Safe)** where it must store all the video recordings of what people do when they use accounts on that platform.

In your lab on page 100, you are setting this for the **Lin Logon 30** platform to ensure that its recordings don't get mixed up with others.

### 2. How does it work?

When a user clicks "Connect" to log into a computer, CyberArk starts its "security camera" (the PSM) to record the session. When the session ends, CyberArk looks at the platform's settings:

- It checks the `SessionRecorderSafe` value.
- It sees the name you typed there (like `PSMR_LinLogon30`).
- It automatically puts the finished recording file into that specific Safe.

We do this systematically (naming it after the Platform ID) because if a company has thousands of recordings, putting them all in one single Safe could make the system slow and make it very hard for a security guard (an Auditor) to find a specific video later.

### 3. Where will sessions be stored?

The sessions will be stored in the **specific Safe named in that parameter**.

For the exercise on page 100, you are changing the value to **`PSMR_LinLogon30`**. This means any time someone uses a Linux logon account, that video is sent straight to the Safe named **PSMR_LinLogon30** inside the Vault.
