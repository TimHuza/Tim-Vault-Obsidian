#cyberark-labs 


Think of a **Logon Account** as a **"Helper Key"** or a **"Front Door Key"** that CyberArk uses when a main account isn't allowed to open a door by itself. Its primary purpose is to help the CyberArk robot (the CPM) get into a system to perform work when the main account is restricted.

Here are the two main reasons we use them:

- **To Get Through a Restricted Door:** For security, many computers (like Linux servers) do not allow the most powerful "Master Key" (the **root** account) to log in directly from the outside. In this case, CyberArk uses a **Logon Account** to log in as a "normal person" first. Once it is inside the "front door," the system can then "jump" or switch to the powerful root account to do its job.
- **To Bridge "Different Worlds":** Sometimes an account belongs to one system (like an **Oracle Database**) but needs to change a file on a different system (like a **Linux server**). The database account doesn't have a "badge" to enter the Linux server, so CyberArk uses a **Logon Account** that lives on the Linux server to open the door and let the robot update the necessary files.

In short, a Logon Account creates a relationship where **CyberArk uses Account A (the helper) to reach and control Account B (the main account)**. This is necessary because many important accounts **cannot manage themselves** or are blocked from logging in directly for safety.
